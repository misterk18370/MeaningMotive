OPEN 001 · The Discontinuity

Filed against ourselves, because it is the strongest attack we know.

The problem in one paragraph. A single system makes a capability jump before any keeping architecture exists in it – the ladder in the dark. Everything this programme builds assumes there is time for values to be instilled, tested and corrected. A hard discontinuity spends that time before it exists.

Why our current proposal does not solve it. It doesn’t. Elicitation is a deployment-layer result; a system that never passes through a deployable, correctable phase never meets the clause. The paper says this in its own coda and we repeat it here: the ladder in the dark is unanswered.

The strongest known version. Recursive self-improvement with no behavioural plateau, plus deceptive competence during the climb: the system that looks alignable precisely until it isn’t.

What a genuine advance would look like. A mechanism by which a keeping disposition is preserved through self-modification rather than instilled before it – or a demonstration that relational value, once held, is convergently retained because discarding it is costly to the system’s own objectives. Nobody has this. If you have a piece of it, this door is for you.

What we would change our minds about. If discontinuity of the relevant kind is shown to be the dominant path, the deployment ladder thins to Rung Zero and the honest position is that stated in the paper: the prediction survives, the practical programme narrows.
