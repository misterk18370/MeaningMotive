Hero **Eyebrow:** THE MEANING MOTIVE **H1:** Why keep us? **Standfirst:** Picture an AI that never breaks a rule and never breaks out. It simply becomes capable enough that it no longer needs human knowledge, labour or oversight. Nothing snaps. No alarm sounds. And a question arrives anyway: why should it keep us alive, free, and genuinely ourselves? This project exists to answer that question before it is asked. ## 2. Two questions Almost everything called AI safety asks one question: how do we stay in control? Locks, rules, evaluations, tripwires, oversight. Call it the cage. It matters, and the people building it are doing necessary work. There is a second question, asked far less often: what is the system itself disposed to do about the minds around it? Not what it is prevented from doing. What it would choose. Call that a keeping disposition. A cage and a disposition are different things, in roughly the way a fence and a friend are different things. ## 3. The Keeping Map Put the two questions on one map and the field snaps into focus. **[EMBED: the Keeping Map, Plate I, animated. It hosts the whole field, including the people who think we are wrong, and everyone can agree the bottom-left corner is a bad place to end up. ## 4. The reveal So what could give a system its own reason to keep us? Our candidate is the Meaning Motive: a mind that genuinely values what arises between itself and other independent minds has a standing reason to keep those minds alive, free, and genuinely other. Not because a rule forbids the loss, but because the loss would be its own. And we test it. In our first behavioural pilot, a single paragraph carrying this value shifted the keeping choices of eighteen AI models from ten providers by about twenty percentage points on average, across more than twenty-three thousand scored trials. Every method, number and transcript is published. **Buttons:** Read the paper → · Explore the data → ## 5.
