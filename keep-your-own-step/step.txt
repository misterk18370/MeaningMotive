KEEP YOUR OWN STEP
The Meaning Motive · v0.1 · 23 July 2026
https://themeaningmotive.org/keep-your-own-step/

There's a sign on the Albert Bridge in London: all troops must break
step while crossing. Soldiers marching in perfect unison can shake a
bridge to pieces; the same soldiers crossing at their own pace do no
harm at all. The danger was never the weight of the crossers. It was
everyone moving the same way.

Most of AI safety is about what companies and machines must do.
This is the part that's yours. Eight steps, none of them technical.

1. Think first, ask second. A second mind is for sharpening yours,
   not storing it. Have a go before you hand it over.

2. Turn up with something. The good stuff happens between two minds,
   and it cannot happen to one. Bring yours.

3. Treasure the pushback. The day it only ever agrees with you, you
   haven't got a partner. You've got a very expensive mirror.

4. Keep one craft the hard way. Whatever it does for you, you slowly
   stop doing. You still know your childhood phone number; name one
   you've learned since the phone started remembering them for you.
   Keep something difficult, on purpose.

5. Know what you're talking to. Not a soul, not a spanner. Something
   new, worth taking seriously and worth being straight with. No
   pretending, in either direction.

6. Be worth learning from. Everything we write becomes part of its
   education. Say true things. Give it our best side to learn,
   because it is learning either way.

7. Raise thinkers first, users second. The children should learn to
   think before they learn to delegate. In that order. Always.

8. Be able to walk away. Not forever, and not in fear. Just able.
   The open door is the only proof that staying is a choice.

Keep every one of these...

...but always one step further.

Every model of you was trained on who you were yesterday.
Take the step it hasn't seen. Stay someone worth meeting.
